Your Rate Card

Daily Output Rate Start with your monthly income target. Divide by the number of days per month you realistically dedicate to this type of work. That's your Daily Output Rate. Factor in your experience level, the complexity of work you typically handle, and what you'd charge a direct client today.

Your Daily Output Rate is not your take-home figure — it's the baseline we use to build your quote. A formula applied to your rate accounts for the complexity, context, and scale of each brief. What you earn per project will typically exceed your stated rate.
